## Entry 1 — Brand Story
**Entry Type:** Brand Story · **Status:** Published · **Owner:** Jacob · **Tags:** origin, positioning

Heart Magic exists because the drink itself does something specific: it opens the heart to love, creativity, and connection with yourself. The name isn't decorative — it's the mechanism. That opening is what leads to dancing, singing, deep conversation, meditating, journaling, or simply working from a present, energized place instead of a wired, anxious one.

The brand's tagline, **"Ancient Power, Modern Magic,"** is the whole positioning in four words: a centuries-old ceremonial cacao tradition (Mayan in origin, heirloom-genetics cacao sourced from small Peruvian farms) made frictionless for modern life. No chopping blocks, no blender, no mess — melt a heart into hot water and drop in. The ritual survives; the barriers to actually doing it every day don't.

Positioning summary from the brand pack: **"sacred indulgence"** — premium wellness that feels personal, not clinical. Warm cream and deep red evoke comfort and intimacy; the goal is to feel like a warm cup held in both hands, not a supplement label.

## Entry 2 — Voice & Tone
**Entry Type:** Voice & Tone · **Status:** Published · **Owner:** Jacob · **Tags:** copywriting, voice

**Is:** Warm, inviting, playful, casual. Speaks to the heart, not the head. Spiritual without being preachy. Real — grounded in an actual ritual and actual sourcing, not hype.

**Is not:** Loud, salesy, frantic, or overly polished. Not futuristic or "AI brand" in feel. Not biohacking/productivity-hack language. Not oversold as a super-stimulant.

**The 70/30 rule** (from the Ambassador Talking Points doc, directly applicable to all copy, not just ambassador content): 70% story, problem, and emotional context — describe life *before* Heart Magic. 30% solution — how it actually changed the morning, the energy, the state. Personal story always outperforms a features list.

**A real example line from the brand pack**, worth using as a tone calibration reference: *"Wanna Get Your Mystery Discount?"* — casual, a little playful, never stiff.

*Reference: Brand Guidelines for Amazon (Drive); Heart Magic Ambassador Talking Points 2026 (Drive)*

## Entry 3 — Messaging Pillars
**Entry Type:** Messaging Pillars · **Status:** Published · **Owner:** Jacob · **Tags:** positioning, copywriting

1. **Frictionless ritual.** A cup in about 20 seconds — no blender, no clumps, no mess. The barrier to a "beautiful ritual in theory" is always real life being busy; this removes the barrier.
2. **Calm, grounded energy — no crash.** Not fake stimulation. Theobromine and functional mushrooms (Cordyceps for oxygen/ATP delivery, plus Lion's Mane, Reishi, Chaga, Maitake, Golden Oyster) support steady energy and nervous-system balance, not a caffeine spike-and-crash. There is a small amount of caffeine — the messaging should never claim zero, only that it isn't the primary mechanism.
3. **Ancient tradition, honored, not costume.** Rooted in real Mayan ceremonial cacao culture; heirloom genetics from small Peruvian farms (Piura region specifically for the fruity, bright flavor profile, distinct from earthier Guatemalan-style varietals). Sourcing honors the farmers and the land, not just the ingredient.
4. **Come back to yourself.** The emotional core, distinct from "wake yourself up": a morning boundary before emails, texts, and the world pull on you.
5. **A real brand for real people.** Explicitly not a supplement-hype or biohacking brand — purchased more by women than men, grounded in real photography and real ingredients over AI-generated polish.

*Reference: Heart Magic Ambassador Talking Points 2026 (Drive); Brand Guidelines for Amazon (Drive)*

## Entry 4 — Do's & Don'ts
**Entry Type:** Do's & Don'ts · **Status:** Published · **Owner:** Jacob · **Tags:** creative-direction, photography

**Do:**
- Use real people, real emotions, natural light, real stock photography (e.g., an actual Lion's Mane mushroom photo, not an AI-generated one).
- Earth tones, natural materials (real wood, earth-tone fabric) as backdrops.
- Anchor visuals on the deep red from the flagship product — "everything can be modeled off of this product."
- Use the oyster-mushroom image and the red-with-gold flower-of-life pattern as background motifs when a photographic background isn't right.
- Photography of the ritual itself — cacao being poured or shared, mugs and glasses — is the core visual language, not product-on-white.
- A feminine essence, even when presenting numbers/stats.

**Don't:**
- Don't use AI-generated imagery except when genuinely necessary — this is a real, potent earth medicine, and the brand should look like one.
- Don't present as futuristic, an "AI brand," a get-rich-quick supplement, a productivity hack, or a super-stimulant.
- Don't present the cacao as highly processed — it should always read as whole, ceremonial-grade, minimally intervened.
- Don't go loud, salesy, or frantic in tone (see Voice & Tone, Entry 2).

*Reference: Brand Guidelines for Amazon (Drive)*

## Entry 5 — Visual Identity Reference

| Accent / highlights | Warm Gold | `#D4A574` |
| Page background | Warm Cream | `#F0E6D3` |
| Secondary text / captions | Warm Gray | `#878787` |
| Body text | Black | `#000000` |

**Typography:**
- **Alice** (serif, weight 400) — logo, headings, hero text.
- **Poppins** (weights 300–600) — body copy, buttons, navigation, product info.
- **Josefin Sans** (weights 300–700) — labels, section headers, navigation accents.

**Logo usage:** primary version is the "Heart Magic" wordmark in Alice, cream/white on deep red background; monotone black-and-white versions are approved and work cleanly due to simple letterforms; minimum digital width 120px.

**Brand personality keywords:** Warm, Ceremonial, Organic, Feminine, Artisanal.

**Target audience (as stated in the brand pack):** wellness-conscious women 25–45 interested in ceremonial cacao, mindfulness, and heart-opening rituals; values ethically sourced, artisanal products.

## A Decision This Surfaces

The Design System document (Sprint 1) used an invented clay/terracotta palette and Fraunces/Inter typography, because no real brand file existed to work from yet. Now that one does, there's a real gap: the invented system and the actual brand share a sensibility (warm, organic, premium) but not the same hex values or typefaces. The Tailwind config, component tokens, and HTML style guide already delivered are all built on the invented palette.

Worth deciding explicitly rather than letting it drift: should I reconcile the Design System doc (and the style-guide specimen) to the real Deep Red / Warm Gold / Warm Cream palette and Alice / Poppins / Josefin Sans typefaces now, before any more UI gets built on the wrong values?
